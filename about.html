About me

My journey began in July 2024 when I started exploring AI tools to support my father's business. Watching practical tutorials and step-by-step guides convinced me that learning Python would be the fastest way to build useful tools. I committed to daily practice and focused learning: small, consistent steps that compound over time.

I learned from the book Python Crash Course by Eric Matthes and supplemented that with math and statistics lessons from Khan Academy. I followed a practice-first approach inspired by Atomic Habits: daily progress, measurable practice, and consistent review.

I then completed several Kaggle micro-courses (Intro to ML, Intermediate ML, Feature Engineering) and began taking part in Kaggle Playground competitions. Those experiences gave me exposure to real-world problems and taught me how to iterate quickly on models and features.

Today, I am focused on machine learning, AI, and clear visual storytelling. I believe data is more than numbers — every dataset has a story. My goal is to turn those stories into insights and practical decisions that help people and organizations.
